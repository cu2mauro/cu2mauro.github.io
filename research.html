Research

Phase-transition dynamics and gravitational waves from binary neutron-star mergers

My current research focuses on the dynamics of phase transitions in dense nuclear matter and their imprint on the gravitational-wave signal of binary neutron-star (BNS) mergers. I work within the CRC-TR 211 collaboration in Frankfurt, combining numerical relativity / GRMHD simulations with holographic models of strongly-coupled dense matter.

Bubble dynamics in first-order transitions

We investigate the nucleation, growth and collision of bubbles of a new phase in special-relativistic hydrodynamic (SRHD) simulations, including subgrid corrections to the energy–momentum tensor that capture the unresolved interface dynamics. The aim is a faithful three-dimensional description of the nucleation history relevant for merger remnants. See, for instance, Bea et al., arXiv:2412.09588.

MHz gravitational waves from BNS mergers

Using GRMHD codes from the FUKA / FIL / GRACE / BHAC+ pipeline, together with a subgrid model for the phase-transition dynamics, we aim at the first accurate prediction of the MHz tail of the gravitational-wave spectrum from a binary neutron-star merger, and at assessing its observability with future ultra-high-frequency GW detectors.

Holographic equation of state

The equation of state of dense matter is supplied by a top-down holographic model, providing a model-independent handle on the high-density, strongly-coupled regime that is otherwise inaccessible both to perturbative QCD and to standard nuclear-physics methods.

Past research — string theory and holography

During my Ph.D. and M.Sc. in Florence I worked on the gauge / gravity duality, with a particular focus on confinement and the phase structure of strongly-coupled gauge theories. The main directions:

Model-independent tools

A large part of my work used probes that can be defined in any holographic background, independently of the specific top-down construction:

  • Wilson loops as diagnostics of confinement and screening, including the holographic computation of the static quark–antiquark potential — see Giliberti, Fatemiabhari & Nunez, JHEP 11 (2024) 068.
  • Entanglement entropy via the Ryu–Takayanagi prescription, as a complementary order parameter for confinement / deconfinement transitions — see Chatzis, Fatemiabhari, Giliberti & Hammond, arXiv:2509.19434.
  • Thermal holography: free energy, equation of state and phase diagrams of strongly-coupled gauge theories at finite temperature and density.

Top-down constructions

I also worked on specific top-down setups built from wrapped branes and quiver gauge theories, comparing their predictions with the Witten–Sakai–Sugimoto (WSS) model. These constructions provide a controlled embedding in string theory and allow one to track how the general lessons drawn from model-independent tools survive in concrete UV-complete examples.

For an excellent introduction to gauge theories and confinement, I recommend D. Tong's gauge theory lecture notes.
